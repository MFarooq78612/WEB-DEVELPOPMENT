Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936-2021

Father of Pakistan's atomic weapons program

After years of house arrest, Khan successfully filed a lawsuit against the Federal Government of Pakistan at the Islamabad High Court whose verdict declared his debriefing unconstitutional and freed him on 6 February 2009.[11][12] The United States reacted negatively to the verdict and the Obama administration issued an official statement warning that Khan still remained a "serious proliferation risk".[13] After his death on 10 October 2021, he was given a state funeral at Faisal Mosque before being buried at the H-8 graveyard in Islamabad.

Biographics

  • Born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, British India (now in Madhya Pradesh, India).
  • Moved to Pakistan in 1952 after the partition of India.
  • Studied at Karachi University and later pursued a degree in metallurgical engineering from the Technical University of Berlin, Germany.
  • Returned to Pakistan in 1972 after completing his education.
  • Joined Pakistan's nuclear program in the 1970s.
  • Played a crucial role in the development of Pakistan's nuclear weapons.
  • Controversially accused of involvement in nuclear proliferation by allegedly sharing nuclear technology with other countries, including North Korea, Libya, and Iran.
  • Considered a national hero in Pakistan for his contributions to the country's nuclear capabilities.
  • Instrumental in the successful conduct of Pakistan's nuclear tests, known as Chagai-I, on May 28, 1998.
